100 km Populaire

Ride: 100 km Populaire
Organizers: Tom Lawrence
Date: Saturday, February 25, 2006
Time: 9:00 a.m. (Registration will close at 8:50 - no exceptions!)
Start Location: Redhook Brewery, Woodinville, WA
Address: 14300 N.E. 145th St. Woodinville, WA 98072
Entry Fee: None

Notes: The ride will be run as a mini-brevet, complete with control cards. Please bring a completed registration form to the start, but there is no fee and everyone is welcome. If you need to join SIR or RUSA for the upcoming brevet series, please bring those forms and payment as well. Fenders are a highly recommended or you'll face the scorn and abuse of your fellow Randonneurs!

Reminder: All riders are reminded that any failure to conduct the ride in accordance with SIR Brevet Rider Expectations, RUSA rules, applicable laws, or any special instructions or equipment requirements for this ride may result in a time penalty or disqualification.

Entry Forms:

  • 100 km Entry Form
  • SIR Membership Form

Route Description: The reverse course of the last few years: This course is a chance for those familiar with the Snoqualmie Valley to get reacquainted with popular cycling routes and a good introduction for the uninitiated. From the brewery, we head up the Sammamish River Trail to beautiful downtown Woodinville and up north into Snohomish County. Turning west, it's up and over High Bridge Road and into the Snoqualmie Valley. Across the valley to Duvall, we head up through Cherry Valley before the control at Sandy's in Carnation. After passing the research farm, we head up over Union Hill to loosen up the legs before touring through Marymoor Park and onto the Trail for the last stretch back to the brewery.

End of the ride: Can you say "beer"? After nearly 3900 feet of climbing, of course you can.
