SIR 2004 200 km

Brevet: 200 km

Organizer: Greg Cox
Date: Saturday, March 20, 2004
Time: 7:00 a.m.

Start Location: Greg Cox's house
Phone: 253-639-2928
Address: 24308 147th Ave SE, Kent WA 98042

Directions:

From the north: Take SR 167 south to the Exit marked 84th Ave S. to N Central Ave. Go Left from the Exit for 0.9 miles. Go Left on James ST UP the hill. Travel approx. 4 miles to 144th Ave SE. Go right on 144th Ave SE (Entrance to Ridgefield neighborhood). Go 0.1 mile to park on the right.

From the south: Take SR 167 or I-5 north to Hwy 18 East. take the Green River Community College exit (first exit after crossing the Green River.) Travel 0.2 mile and go right at the T intersection. Go 0.3 mile to the first light and go right onto 132nd Ave. Take 132nd approx 4 miles to 240th. Go right on 240th (QFC on corner) and travel 6 miles to 144th Ave SE go right on 144th Ave SE (Entrance to Ridgefield neighborhood). Go 0.1 mile to park on the right.

Entry Fee: $10 SIR members, $20 non-members. Please make all entry and membership fees payable to SIR. Medals are $7 and are only available to SIR members.

Reminder: All riders are reminded that any failure to conduct the ride in accordance with SIR Brevet Rider Expectations, RUSA rules, applicable laws, or any special instructions or equipment requirements for this ride may result in a time penalty or disqualification.

Please download and fill out this Entry Form in advance.

  • 200 km Entry Form (HTML)

Please fill out the SIR Membership Form BEFORE the ride if you are joining SIR. Please also try to have correct change (or a check) for the entry fee, medal, SIR annual fee, and RUSA annual fee.

Special Instructions: This is the first official distance brevet of the year so please be prepared. Fenders are a must or you'll face the scorn and abuse of your fellow Randonneurs! This is a good time to try out lights, bags, racks, food, etc. Please note that this year's ride is earlier in the season and the probable overcast skies makes the potential for finishing in the dark much higher than in past years. ALL Riders are STRONGLY encouraged to bring a front light and are REQUIRED to have a rear light!

Route Description: The perennial favorite 200 km ride and chili feed returns again in 2004 with a brand new route. Start the morning with a brisk jaunt to Black Diamond and its famous bakery. From the bakery, the ride travels out to Hobart and then to Ennumclaw. From Ennumclaw the ride travels out to Greenwater on  410.  Wave to the other riders as you descend 410 to Mud Mt. Dam.  The route then takes you to Carbonato and Orting before sending back to Kent. 

End of the ride: Please join us (spouses welcome) for an end of the ride party. We will have home made chili, snacks, refreshments, etc. If you want to clean up after the ride, please bring your own towel.
