100 km Populaire

Ride: 100 km Populaire
Organizers: Tom Lawrence
Date: Saturday, March 5, 2005
Time: 9:00 a.m. (Registration will close at 8:50 - no exceptions!)
Start Location: Redhook Brewery, Woodinville, WA
Address: 14300 N.E. 145th St. Woodinville, WA 98072
Entry Fee: None

Notes: The ride will be run as a mini-brevet, complete with control cards. Please bring a completed registration form to the start, but there is no fee and everyone is welcome. If you need to join SIR or RUSA for the upcoming brevet series, please bring those forms and payment as well. Fenders are a highly recommended or you'll face the scorn and abuse of your fellow Randonneurs!

Reminder: All riders are reminded that any failure to conduct the ride in accordance with SIR Brevet Rider Expectations, RUSA rules, applicable laws, or any special instructions or equipment requirements for this ride may result in a time penalty or disqualification.

Entry Forms:

  • 100 km Entry Form
  • SIR Membership Form

Route Description: This course is a chance for those familiar with the Snoqualmie Valley to get reacquainted with popular cycling routes and a good introduction for the uninitiated. From the brewery, we tackle a few gratuitous hills on the way to the West Snoqualmie Valley Road. Great riding in the valley to the first control at Sandy's Espresso in Carnation. From Sandy's, we head north to Monroe with a detour up through Cherry Valley. After the turnaround at Monroe, we head across the valley to tackle High Bridge Road. Turning on Fales Road, we head south with a few more hills to loosen up the legs. We tour through beautiful downtown Woodinville, turning onto the Sammamish River Trail for the last stretch back to the brewery.

End of the ride: Can you say "beer"? After 2300 feet of climbing, of course you can.
