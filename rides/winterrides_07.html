2007 Winter Ride Series: Le Paysage à Vélo (countryside by bicycle)

Welcome to the ninth year for the Winter Ride Series: Le Paysage à Vélo - truly a Seattle tradition now! The series has been successful with the current format and will be run as in has in the past. All the ride details are listed here and each ride will be listed in the Cascade newsletter. If you forget you can always check the SIR web site. The first ride will be the weekend of January 13th and will lead up to the SIR 100 kilometer event on March 3rd and the SIR 200 kilometer event on March 17th with distances varying from about 35 miles to 85 miles, always with shortcuts available.

These rides are a great way to get in some early season training with a natural buildup of miles while enjoying the company of other riders. Winter riding is tough enough - this gives you a chance to do it with friends. You know what they say - misery loves company. It's also a great way to meet local randonneurs and learn about randonneuring.

Please welcome Peter Beeson this year as official co-series leader! Peter has been a ride leader for Cascade and an avid randonneur for several years and has helped with this series in the past. Beginning next year Peter will the leader as I'll be passing the series to him. Thanks for your help Peter.

Details: The rides are listed below. The rides start in various parts of the Seattle area, from Auburn to Stanwood and Conway. This will hopefully give people in different areas a chance to ride near an area they are familiar or give those not familiar with the area a chance to see some new roads.

  • We'll start all rides at 9:00am on Saturday. This doesn't mean show up and gather at 9am, this means we leave at 9am, so be sure to show up a few minutes earlier to pump up your tires, adjust your fenders, and put all your warm clothes on. Please note that some rides involve ferries, so arrive a bit earlier.
  • All rides are rain or shine. We might shorten the ride for really, really bad weather. The only cancellation might be for snow or ice.
  • We generally stop infrequently and briefly. We like to keep warm and keep moving. As a result, we don't regroup very often and it's usually fairly spontaneous when it happens. This means you need to be self-sufficient to some extent.
  • Usually the mid-group pace averages 15mph including hills and flats. The faster folks are usually closer to 17mph while the back of the back can be closer to 12mph. The pace may vary depending on terrain, weather and participants.
  • While each ride has a designed distance, there are almost always shortcuts to get back. There are also almost always ways to extend the ride as well. Just ask and I can point you to where you want to go.
  • Cue sheets of the route will be provided on the day of the ride and on the website. If you aren't familiar with the area or the route, I suggest getting a local map and looking up the route. You will need to provide your own plastic bag to keep the map dry :-)
  • Please have fenders with a mud flap as they are a must on days when the roads are wet. Forgetting once is forgivable. Forgetting twice - well expect some harassment :-)
  • Please have sufficient riding skills and experience to ride in a group or a paceline. And as always please respect traffic laws and local traffic.

For this year there are some classic routes and some new ones. We'll cover a lot of territory as we explore the Puget Sound area!
